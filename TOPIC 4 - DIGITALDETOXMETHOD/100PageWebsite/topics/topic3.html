How to Do a Social Media Cleanse

A social media cleanse involves taking a break from platforms like Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, or TikTok to regain mental clarity and reduce stress. This can be a short-term break or a long-term shift in how you use social media.

Why Consider a Social Media Cleanse?

  • Reduce Comparison: Constant exposure to curated content can lead to unhealthy self-comparison and lower self-esteem.
  • Improve Mental Health: A cleanse can decrease anxiety, FOMO (fear of missing out), and feelings of isolation.
  • Boost Productivity: Less time scrolling frees up time and attention for meaningful activities.

7 steps to Social Media Cleanse

  1. Turn off notifications or switch to Do Not Disturb mode to lock in and avoid constant pings.
  2. Untag old and unnecessary photos that no longer reflect who you are or serve a purpose.
  3. Unfollow or unfriend people who annoy, disturb, or bring negativity to your feed.
  4. Minimize your usage by limiting the time spent on apps or using screen time controls.
  5. Leave uninspiring or unproductive groups on platforms like Facebook or WhatsApp.
  6. Unfollow distracting conversations that might derail your progress during the cleanse.
  7. Inform your friends that you're taking a break to set expectations and avoid pressure to respond.

After the Cleanse

  • Reflect: Journal your experience—how did it feel? What did you miss (or not miss)?
  • Reintroduce Mindfully: If you return, set time limits or unfollow accounts that don’t serve you.
  • Establish Boundaries: Consider regular social media-free hours, days, or app timers.
